A 58 year old woman with a history of primary hyperparathyroidism.

Parathyroid Carcinoma

The arterial phase CT neck images show a large 3 cm mass posterior to the right thyroid lobe with a few internal calcifications; the mass is hypoenhancing relative to the adjacent thyroid tissue. The sestamibi scan shows prominent uptake in the nodule on delayed images. Ultrasound shows a homogenous hypoechoic round nodule.

Fine needle aspiration of the nodule prior to surgery was negative for malignancy and showen parathyroid tissue. The patient was scheduled for a right thyroid lobectomy and parathyroidectomy. The intraoperative frozen sections were consistent with parathyroid carcinoma.

Size predicts the likelihood of malignancy in parathyroid nodules: nodules measuring less than 15 mm are almost certainly benign1. In nodules measuring greater than 15 mm, infiltration and calcification have a very high (reportedly 100%) positive predictive value for malignancy.

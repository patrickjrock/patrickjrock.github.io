Patrick Rock's Sailing Resume


Summary

I grew up sailing small boats on a small lake in Texas and have been participating in charters since 2023. I enjoy coastal crusing, traditional navigation, and knots.

Organizations

  • Latrappe Creek Historical and Ecological Socitey
  • The Center for Wooden Boats, Seattle
  • American Sailing Association ID: 2024220135

Certifications

  • ASA 101, Keelboat Sailing 1, completed June 2024 with South Coast Sailing on a Catalina 22 (Fire Water) in Galveston Bay.
  • ASA 103, Basic Coastal Cruising, completed July 2024 with Puget Sound Sailing on a Tartan 37 (Ørn) in Puget's Sound. We sailed out of Des Moines and practiced man overboard recovery, picking up mooring balls and anchoring.
  • ASA 104, Bareboat Cruising, completed June 2025 with San Juan Sailing on a Jeanneau 45 (Misty Blue Yonder) in the San Juan Islands. We sailed to Suicia island, anchored in echo bay, and then back to Bellingham. We practiced paper chart navigation, and we rigged a preventer.

Sailing Experience

September 9-15, 2023: Crewed on "Eos", a Benatau 37, for 7 days, 200 nautical miles, in the upper Chesapeake Bay with Tom Ferone (captain) and Tom Joint for the Latrappe Creek 50th anniversary expedition. Ports of call and anchorages included: Annapolis, Rock Hall, West River Galesville, Wye River, St. Michaels, Oxford, and Latrappe Creek.

June 11, 15, 22, 2023: Three days of sailing on "Fire Water", a Catalina 22, 50 nautical miles total, in Galveston Bay with Nate Shane out of Seabrook.

September 15-26, 2024: Crewed on "Juna", a Bavaria 46, for 11 days, 500 nautical miles, in the Baltic Sea and Danish Straits, with Bob Vanderlinde (captain), Roger Edgley, and Mike Piccirillo for the Latrappe Creek 51st Expedition. Notable experiences included sailing with a gennaker for two days, and an overnight 60 nm sail from Klintholmhavn to Warnemünde. Ports of call included: Warnemünde, Burgtiefe, Spodsbjerg, Nyborg, Ballen, Sejerø, Hundested, Helsingør, Copenhagen, and Klintholmhavn.

Wednesday night tomato sauce

Four servings

Add 1 tablespoon of olive oil to an enameled cast iron pot, and turn the heat to medium until the oil is heated. Remove the casings from 5 italian sausages and add them to the pot. Allow the sausage to brown for 5 minutes; while it is browning, break it apart with a wooden spoon. After the sausage has some color, add one diced onion and cook until it is translucent. Pour in 1, 28 oz can of San Marzano tomatoes and deglaze the pot with the juices. Season with: 1-2 tsp of red pepper flakes, a few grids of black pepper, and a pinch of salt. If they are available, add a spoonful of capers. Allow this to simmer, on medium low, uncovered, for 45 minutes. Consider adding a glug of wine if the sauce needs hydration. Finally, season to taste.

Cook the pasta (I would reccomend a penne or farfalle) according to box directions, drain, and then stir the noodles into the sauce pot. Usually an entire 16 oz box of pasta will be too much for this amount of sauce and will give a suboptimal pasta-to-sauce ratio.

Serve with freshly grated parmesian or pecorino, and freshly chopped parsley.

Patrick Rock's Sailing Resume

Summary

I grew up sailing small boats on lakes in Texas and have been participating in charters since 2023. I enjoy cruising, traditional navigation, and knots.

  • Total sailing days: 37 (10 as skipper)
  • Total distance: 975 nautical miles
  • Boat types: Monohulls, 20–46 ft
  • Regions: Chesapeake Bay, Galveston Bay, Baltic Sea, Salish Sea

Organizations

  • Latrappe Creek Historical and Ecological Society
  • The Center for Wooden Boats, Seattle
  • American Sailing Association ID: 2024220135

Certifications

  • ASA 101, Keelboat Sailing: completed June 2024 with South Coast Sailing on a Catalina 22 (Fire Water) in Galveston Bay.
  • ASA 103, Basic Coastal Cruising: completed July 2024 with Puget Sound Sailing on a Tartan 37 (Ørn) in Puget Sound. Practiced man overboard recovery, mooring, and anchoring.
  • ASA 104, Bareboat Cruising: completed June 2025 with San Juan Sailing on a Jeanneau 45 (Misty Blue Yonder) in the San Juan Islands. Practiced paper chart navigation and rigged a preventer.

Sailing Log

  • September 9–15, 2023

    Crewed on Eos, a Beneteau 37, for 7 days and 200 nautical miles in the upper Chesapeake Bay with Tom Ferone (skipper) and Tom Joint for the Latrappe Creek 50th Anniversary Expedition. Ports of call: Annapolis, Rock Hall, Galesville, Wye River, St. Michaels, Oxford, Latrappe Creek.

  • June 11, 15, 22, 2023

    Day sailed as skipper on Fire Water, a Catalina 22, over 3 days (50 nautical miles) in Galveston Bay with Nate Shane out of Seabrook.

  • September 15–26, 2024

    Crewed on Juna, a Bavaria 46, for 11 days and 500 nautical miles in the Baltic Sea and Danish Straits with Bob Vanderlinde (skipper), Roger Edgley, and Mike Piccirillo (mate) for the Latrappe Creek 51st Expedition. Highlights: Gennaker sailing, 60 nm overnight passage, and repairing a leak in the freshwater system. Ports of call: Warnemünde, Burgtiefe, Spodsbjerg, Nyborg, Ballen, Sejerø, Hundested, Helsingør, Copenhagen, Klintholmhavn.

  • December 8,9 2024

    Day sailed on Pamela a Blanchard Junior Knockabout on Lake Union, Seattle, at the center for wooden boats. One day crewed with Christian Holtz and one skippered with Jack Kuipers. The lake also serves as an airport for seaplanes.

  • May 5, 2025

    Day sailed on a Catalina 30 out of Shilshole Bay Marina with Dominic Suchu (skipper), Sandor Fogassy, and Louise Fogassy. Light winds.

  • July 5-11, 2025

    Skippered on Amazing Grace, a Bavaria 37, for 7 days and 200 nm in the San Juan and Gulf Islands with Priya Rock, Nate Shane, Brian Zhu, and James Bowman. First experience as skipper on a weeklong charter. Highlights: Crossing Haro strait in moderate wind, clearing customs into Canada and back, navigating Sansum narrows during flood tide. Ports of call: Squalicum Harbor, Echo Bay, Bedwell Harbor, Ganges, Telegraph Harbor, Sidney, Friday Harbor, Blakely Island, and Pelican Beach.

  • July 24, 2025

    Skippered on a Catalina 22 out of Dillon Marina on Lake Dillon, Colorado with Rich Rock, Priya Rock and Kevin Kichham. Challenging weather with frequently shifting winds due to the surrounding montain ranges. The marina was 9,017 feet above sea level.

Objectives

  • Identify on a chest radiograph the anatomic locations of the lobes and fissures of the lungs and the lobar location of typical bacterial pneumonias.
  • Recognize the typical appearance of a pleural effusion and signs of a pneumothorax on upright and supine radiographs.
  • Describe and recognize the typical appearance of volume loss on chest radiographs and its significance, including lobar atelectasis.
  • Define the appropriate role for CT in suspected chest/abdomen/pelvis trauma.
  • Identify the common radiographic and CT imaging findings seen in traumatic aortic injury.
  • Describe air bronchogram and recognize deep sulcus sign, tension pneumothorax, and skin fold mimics of a pneumothorax on chest radiographs.
  • Recognize correct and incorrect positions of chest tubes, endotracheal tubes and enteric tubes on chest radiographs.
  • List four imaging findings of pulmonary edema and recognize the typical changes of emphysema and COPD on chest radiographs.

Objectives are taken from the official MRAD800 course syllavus.

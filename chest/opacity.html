Opacity

The brightness or darkness of an object is its opacity. Opacity is determined by the absorbtion or attenuation of the X-ray beam. Structres that are more opaque have a higher attenuation.

We roughly divide the opacity of structures into five levels arranged from lowest to highest attenuation:
  • air
  • fat
  • soft tissue
  • bone
  • metal
When reading cross sectional imaging we can directly and quantitatively assess the attenuation of structures using Hounsfield Units.

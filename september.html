COPD

If the airway is obstructed then the FVC (maximal expiration) will decrease and the FEV1 will decrease more. Therefore the FEV1 to FVC ratio will decrease. FEV1/FVC represents the rate at which you can exhale. TLC is increased in COPD due to the trapping of air within the lung. Chronic bronchitis is a common form of COPD. The reid index is defined as the ratio of the thickness of the mucousal glands to the thickness of the airway wall. Smoking and chronic bronchitis cause the reid index to increase above 0.5. Cor pulmonale is caused by hypoxic vasoconstricion leading to right ventricular hypertrophy and right heart failure.

Musculoskeletal Pathology

CREST scleroderma is a group of conditions associated with anticentromere antibodies. CREST stands for: Calcinosis, Raynaud phenomenon, Esophageal dysmotility, Sclerodactyly, and Telangestica. HLA-B27 is assocated with seronegative spondyloarthropathies PAIR standing for Psoriatic arthritis, Ankylosing spondylitis, Inflammatory bowel disease, and Reactive arthritis.
